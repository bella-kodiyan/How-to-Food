Japan

Utensils

The main utensils used in Japan are chopsticks. The use of chopsticks has been part of Japanese culture for a long time, and it remains, to this day, the most commonly used utensil in Japan. It originated from China, when Emperor Suiko sent a delegation to China in order to study their culture. The delegation saw the Chinese people using chopsticks and brought them back to Japan. It is said that the original purpose for starting to use chopsticks was to be able to invite Chinese envoys to Japan. From there, eating with chopsticks became commonplace within the upper class and soon everybody ate with them.

Dining Etiquette

Japan has a lot of manners and customs that revolve around eating. They are far more strict than most other countries as well. Some examples of the manners and customs that are used in meals are the greetings, "Itadakimasu" and "Gochisosama desita" (Thank you for the food), eating with your hands on the bowl or bowls, and many others that are very specific.
