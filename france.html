France

Utensils

The main utensils used in France are forks, knives, and spoons. They have been part of the french culture for a long time, dating far back into the nobility. They come with a variety of different manners that people are expected to follow when eating in any environment, especially in a formal setting.

Dining Etiquette

French culture revolves a lot around eating and therefore comes with a list of manners and etiquette to follow. It is not super strict but can influence one’s view of you depending on if you follow said etiquette. Some examples of this are: not eating until everyone is seated, not eating until the host starts eating/says everyone can eat, keeping both your hands on the table… There are also specific places where each utensil is placed when setting the table, rules about where you place the bread when you eat, etc.

Common Foods

The French diet is very different from many diets around the world. It incorporates lots of full-fat cheese, butter, bread, fresh fruits and vegetables, small portions of meat, etc. Some common foods are ratatouille(thinly sliced stewed vegetables), boeuf bourguignon(beef cooked in red wine and vegetables), bouillabaisse(fish soup that must include at least 4 types of seafood)...
