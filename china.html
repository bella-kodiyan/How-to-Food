China

Utensils

The main utensils used in China are chopsticks. Chopsticks have been used in China for over 3 millennia. They were originally used solely for cooking and it wasn’t until around 400 A.D that they were shortened and used for eating. Chopsticks originated in China and slowly spread over the entirety of southeast Asia. They are now common place in China and are used to eat almost everything.

Dining Etiquette

China has a lot of dining etiquette that goes along with their meals and they can be quite hard to follow if you don’t know about them. The seating arrangement is one such thing, as the placement of people can say a lot about their status. There are also many table manners that one has to pay attention to. For example, you must always let the older people at the table eat first. The younger ones should not eat until the eldest says “let’s eat” or until they eat first. Overall however, Chinese table manners are very similar to western table manners.

Common Foods

Chinese diet consists of mainly grains, vegetables, fruit, and meat. Dairy products are substituted with tofu and soymilk, due to large amounts of the population being lactose intolerant.
